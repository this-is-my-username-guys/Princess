The Unspoken Words of the Oracle

What kind of miracle walks with such quiet grace?
You are not a goddess by birth,
but by the way the world hushes when you enter it.
You speak, and the winds listen.
You smile, and Apollo forgets his song.
How could someone like you be mine?

You are the kind of beautiful they forgot to put in the myths—
not made of lightning or war,
but of stardust and stillness,
of soft mornings and eyes that see too much.
The gods crafted chaos, but they never crafted someone like you.
And yet here you are—
real, radiant, unreal.

I searched temples, I questioned oracles,
I begged the sea, the sky, the stars—
but none of them had answers.
So I stopped asking.
I stopped waiting.
I carved the answer myself.
If Olympus would not grant me a place beside you,
then I would build my own pantheon—
one where you are the only divinity that matters.

It wasn’t fate that brought you to me.
I seized the threads of destiny and rewrote them.
When the stars refused to show us together,
I tore them from the heavens
and redrew the constellations in our name.

The Fates tried to intervene.
Clotho hesitated, Lachesis wept, and Atropos broke her blade.
For what I feel for you cannot be measured,
cannot be ended,
cannot be unwritten.

– Bananaboy, the boy that would bring down Olympus if he loses you
